Reliance Bank: Mobile Banking App

Role: UX/UI Designer

Timeline: 2 weeks

Tools: Figma, Slack

Idea & Goal

As our FinTech sector continues to grow at a fast pace, they are faced with new problems that have surfaced over recent years. As technology continues to grow, mobile customer support needs to be of higher quality. In addition, the mobile needs of the GenZ and Millennial generations are not being met.

The goal is to create a native mobile banking app that will meet the most common needs of this demographic and build on the functionality based on customer feedback and research in future releases. The banking app is created with two different versions for iOS and Android using their guidelines.

High Level Requirements

1

Customer can securely but easily log into the app

2

Customer can see all banking transactions and balances

3

Customer can pay bills using the app

4

Customers can deposit checks using the app

5

Customers can get notified of important actions on their account

High Fidelity Wireframes

Log Me In

The user can easily and securely log in according to their own preference: typing, face or finger scan, swipe pattern, or a token.

Sound the Alarms

The user will see alerts that need to be checked by the orange indicator on the profile picture.

Pay My Bills

Quickly pay your bills with the "Bills Pay" feature. Finding and adding a company is as easy as signing in.

Where's My Money?!

View recent transactions that are separated by incomes and expenses. Upon viewing all transactions, a visual graph separates the spendings into categories.

Money in the Bank

One feature for depositing a check allows the user to tap anywhere on the screen to take a photo. This inclusive features assists people who are missing a finger/thumb or cannot reach/tap certain areas on their screen.

Three Tabs, One Place

The three way toggle allows for the user to easily change between the different tabs to get a quick view of their expenses and income.

Hello User

The orange circle on the profile picture indicates alerts that needs to be checked. The initial menu gives limited choices of navigation so they're not overwhelmed by a large number of choices upon opening the menu.

Slide to the Left

The orange circle on the profile picture indicates alerts that needs to be checked. The initial menu gives limited choices of navigation so they're not overwhelmed by a large number of choices upon opening the menu.

Tap Anywhere

When depositing a check, the user is able to tap anywhere on the screen to take a photo. This allows accessibility for many users that may have lost fingers or have diffficulty tapping certain areas on their phone.

03DESIGN

Storyboard

Storyboarding allowed me to consider a sequence of states the user may go through. How might my user interact with my product? The actions and emotions involved were my top priority.

Impact/Effort Matrix

This decision-making tool helped me categorize specific feature ideas (while thinking of my MVP prototype). My features were reviewed by a professional developer, Scott B. He assisted in determining the features importance and coding capabilities.

Site Map

Site mapping helps improve website navigation, understanding the scope of work, and avoid missing critical parts of a website’s architecture. During this process, I was able to determine how many screens I may need for my product.

User Flow

Creating a user flow helped form a route map and define all navigation steps in the user journey in order to achieve their goal. This user flow is based on helping the users find assignments that align with their state standards.

04REFINE

Changes

Based on the feedback from the usability testing, I made changes to my design to give users an easy-to-navigate platform. The changes include adding a page header, creating a border around the layout, adding more navigation to the side bar, and creating call to action buttons.
