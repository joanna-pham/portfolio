EdHub: UX Case Study

01OVERVIEW

Background

At the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, I was working as a 1st/2nd grade combo teacher. When we transitioned into virtual learning, there were many complications and confusion for my students, their parents, and my teaching partners. They had frustrations when told to utilize different platforms and assign work from curriculum websites. These sites were hard to navigate, loaded slowly, and had terrible organization. For this project, I wanted to advocate for the users on these different learning platforms because I understood their pain points firsthand.

Challenge

Elementary teachers needed a way to effectively give instructions and assignments to their students during their virtual learning. In a survery sponsored by the University of Phoenix, nine out of ten teachers spend almost as much time preparing for their virtual classes as they did teaching them. How might we re-imagine the online learning platform for elementary teachers during COVID-19?

Objective

For this project, I wanted to develop a course management system for teachers to find pre-made assignments to cut down on preparation time and a way for teachers to communicate with families.

Solution

Elementary teachers need a way to effectively give instructions and assignments to their students. This solution will decrease amount of time spent on the learning platform because teachers need more time to focus on lesson planning to teach virtually.

My Role

UX designer designing a product for teachers from conception to delivery

Site Map

Site mapping helps improve website navigation, understanding the scope of work, and avoid missing critical parts of a website’s architecture. During this process, I was able to determine how many screens I may need for my product.

User Flow

Creating a user flow helped form a route map and define all navigation steps in the user journey in order to achieve their goal. This user flow is based on assisting teachers find assignments that align with their lesson plans so they do not have to recreate assignments.

04REFINE

Changes

Based on the feedback from the usability testing, I made changes to my design to give users an easy-to-navigate platform. The changes include adding a page header, creating a border around the layout, adding more navigation to the side bar, and creating call to action buttons.

05RETROSPECTIVE

Lessons Learned

This has been a positive learning experience for me as this was my first UI/UX project. I was exposed to many different methods throughout the design process to help give the user a positive experience with the product. My next step is to complete my high-fidelity wireframes with the new design and start a second round of usability testing with a different group of my targeted audience. If I decide to pursue this project further, I would design a dashboard for the student side.
